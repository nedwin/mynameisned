Had an incredible wedding in Fitzroy Gardens in Melbourne on March 23 surrounded by friends and family. Work:

We took a valiant swing at it but unfortunately we weren't able to reach escape velocity with Spritz, the corporate card and expense product I spun out of Very Labs. I wrote a little post mortem on it here but the TL;DR is "startups are hard, but I live to fight another day".

I spent all of May in a daze between running an unsuccessful M&A process on Spritz, and acting as entrepreneur in residence for Launch Victoria & Neighbourhood coworking. We ran a series of workshops for incredibly early stage founders to help them turn their side hustle into a full-time gig. It was an incredible crew and very proud to see one of the teams make it into the Melbourne batch of Startmate. Go Alice!

I'm still doing some consulting a couple of days a week with SF & Melbourne-based companies around pricing + product & M&A, while looking for what's next. I'm looking for a great team, at a generational B2B company, where I can flex my product + leadership skills in new ways.

Hit me with your best shot dwyer.ed@gmail.com.

Reading:

The Fish That Ate The Whale: The Life and Times of America's Banana King

Great book on the rise of the banana industry with a focus on the US. Far more espionage than I knew about.

The Wizard of Menlo Park: How Thomas Alva Edison Invented the Modern World

Thomas Edison had a huge impact on the world through his inventions, though by and large he wasn't the one to take the lions share of the value he created.

Interesting to read about his working styles, relationships with folks like Henry Ford, and what's left of his legacy.
